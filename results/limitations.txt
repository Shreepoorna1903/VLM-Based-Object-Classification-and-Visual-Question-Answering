
MODEL LIMITATIONS OBSERVED DURING EVALUATION
=============================================

1. DETR (facebook/detr-resnet-50):
   - High false positive rate on "person" and "bottle" (frequent COCO categories)
   - Poor recall on rare categories (dog: 20% recall with only 5 test instances)
   - Cannot answer questions or provide contextual understanding
   - Limited to the 91 COCO categories it was trained on

2. Qwen2-VL Zero-Shot:
   - Verbose responses make structured metric extraction difficult
   - Very poor counting accuracy (3.4%): tends to describe rather than count
   - Mentions objects outside our 8-category set, inflating false positives
   - Significantly slower than DETR (19x latency)
   - Float16 instability on T4 GPUs required float32 fallback

3. Qwen2-VL Fine-Tuned (QLoRA):
   - Object List F1 decreased slightly (0.50 -> 0.41) suggesting mild overfitting
     or distribution shift in response format
   - Counting accuracy improved with word-number mapping but still low
   - Some responses include grounding tokens (e.g., "objects(221,297),(629,907)")
     indicating the model sometimes confuses tasks
   - Training loss reporting issue: labels included full prompt tokens,
     making average loss appear near zero
   - Limited training data (500 pairs) and epochs (3) constrain improvements

4. General Comparison Limitations:
   - DETR and VLM solve fundamentally different tasks (detection vs VQA),
     making direct metric comparison imperfect
   - VQA accuracy depends heavily on response parsing heuristics
   - 50-image test subset may not capture full performance distribution
   - Category imbalance (person: 72% of annotations) skews overall metrics
